What is A "Web" Standard?

  • A Mix Of Standards (JS) & Recommendations (HTML/CSS)
  • Not All Standards Are Equal
  • Standard Bodies
  • W3C, IETF, ISO, ECMA, Unicode Consortium, IANA
  • Tries To Get Vendors To Agree
  • Standards Making Is Slow Process...
What is a standrd. In this context standards refer to how browsers should render HTML, CSS when you say width="100px" the standard 'recommends' exactly what this should do (More on this on a moment).

Note w3c does not publish strict standards but rather strong recommendations. This is true for HTML & CSS and differs from ECMA. Standards for programming languages are much stricter and should be thought of as rules not recommendation.

Standards can be in different states candidate and recommendation

Let's Talk About Microsoft In The Late 90's and Early 2000's


In the late nineties IE rapidly overtook netscape in market penetration. 1st browser war saw ms throwing money at IE, integrating the browser into their OS and dominating the market.
It was during this time that MS Even though we had an establised method for evolving standards ms went there own way.
For developers this was awful. You needed to spend most of your time developing for the browser with the largest audience. Remember those "Best Viewed in IE badges.
Developing solely for IEs Proprietary standards broke most of the bullets we previously mentioned and would bake in problems later. Even today there are many applications that only work in IE6 because that specific version had a Proprietary feature that was coded too.

IE: Didn't Like To Follow Standards


IE bundles a ton of Proprietary features, ignores W3c recommendations.
imagine coding for ie, you have to now code once for ie and again for other browsers following w3c recs. what happens when there are even more browsers on the market.. or mobile?

Creates A Nightmare For Developers

"Microsoft is committed to implementing the Internet standards that make sense to allow our customers to build great solutions. As standards emerge, we evaluate them to see which standards might best serve our customers' needs."

Someone forgot what the word standard means
Developing cross-browser websites was incredibly painful. The promise of write once run everywhere failed. You had to code for ie and everybody else Who knows if the features you coded against would exist in the future? Supporting multiple versions of IE became even more of a pain. Not just IE vs Netscape but IE6 vs IE7 vs IE8 vs Nestscape
Another case study me. Here I am at my first job out of college happily coding standards compliant HTML & CSS.
And here I am after a few months making fixes for multiple version of IE on the Process & mac. And we didnt even have mobile yet.
